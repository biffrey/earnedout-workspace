1. Executive Summary

"Lean-forward," firm-fixed-price design partner that helps author the requirement pre-PO.
  • Financial highlights: ~$15M revenue, $8–9M EBITDA, ~57–60%+ margin — audited, zero findings, multi-year continuation. ~$17M projected. Owns its HQ.
  • Buy Box summary: CONDITIONAL — size/profitability/tenure pass decisively; documented growth and asking multiple are the open items, both in the SIM.
  • Why pursue: Rare ~60% margin + audited books + long incumbency + exclusive MOB Hub patent license + motivated retirement seller willing to stay; strong fit with Biffrey's DoD footprint.

2. Company Overview

History: Founded 1979 by David Aberizk, P.E. Began in New England federal work — pressure vessels, nuclear power, then cryogenics (GE program / Helix). Relocated to San Diego in the late 1990s; rebuilt around Navy procurement support after 9/11 by embedding in the local defense community (NDIA, SPAWAR buying center). Evolved into a hardware design/qualification partner for aircraft survivability.

Ownership: Founder-owned; David Aberizk is founder, President & CEO and controlling owner. First-ever M&A process (began ~12–18 months ago).

Milestones: 1979 founded · late-1990s San Diego pivot · 2017 MOB Hub/survivability deepening (NAWCWD China Lake) · Dec 2023 HQ purchase · 2024 senior hires (Hill, Surmi) · Jul 2025 exclusive MOB Hub patent license (only non-DoD licensee) · 2025 early EMD delivery to PMA-272 + follow-on SOW through 2029 · 2025–26 fractional CFO, zero-findings audit, SIM, sale process.

Org: Decentralized by design — Aberizk is the technical "maestro"; Hill (CSO/FSO) owns contracts + security; Surmi (VP Military Products) owns client/government relationships; an operations manager runs ops; Fraccinault is fractional CFO. Three TS-cleared staff. Family-culture workforce.

4. Products & Services

What they sell: Ruggedized defense electronics + engineering for aircraft survivability — countermeasure dispensing / programmer hardware, the MOB Hub interface (new-to-legacy integration), directional jammer units, and the next-gen iASE programmer.

Pricing model: Firm-fixed-price with healthy margins; notably pre-PO "lean forward" — ICI helps author the SOW it later bids.

Delivery: Fabless fabricator — in-house design, kitting, and MIL-STD test; machining/board fab/assembly outsourced to trusted SoCal job shops (relationships since 2000). Capital-light, quality-controlled.

Differentiators: Exclusive non-DoD license to four Navy MOB Hub patents; in-house MIL-STD test lab; "speed to fleet" tiger-team agility vs. primes; 47-yr incumbent PMA-272 trust; P.E.-led engineering.

5. Market Analysis

Industry: Defense electronics / electronic warfare & aircraft survivability — a priority, well-funded mission area driven by near-peer threat modernization; counter-cyclical to commercial downturns but exposed to federal appropriations timing.

Drivers: Survivability modernization, obsolescence re-engineering, MOB Hub integration demand, POM-cycle embedment (visibility into 2029), new product lines.

Barriers / headwinds: Annual appropriation risk; clearance + ITAR barriers (also a moat); single-PMA concentration.

Competitive landscape: Competes against primes via tiger-team speed and the fabless model; edge is pre-PO problem-shaping + reliable hardware delivery. ICI's ~60% margin profile is well above typical services-firm norms and should be diligence-verified against audited statements.

6. Customers & Revenue Model

Segments: ~100% U.S. DoD/Navy, anchored by NAVAIR PMA-272. Historical commercial/lab clients are immaterial today.

Retention: High within active programs — follow-on contract authored through 2029; ICI embedded in the customer's POM/budget planning. Risk is concentration, not churn.

Recurring vs one-time: Firm-fixed-price program/hardware deliveries — contracted and repeat, but annually appropriated and milestone-based (not subscription). Scored as mixed.

Concentration: ~100% single-agency, single-PMA — the dominant risk; partially mitigated by multi-year coverage and the seller's continued involvement.
