## 5. Market Analysis

- **Industry size and trends:** HVAC and plumbing services are large, fragmented, non-discretionary home-services markets with steady demand driven by housing stock, replacement cycles, and increasingly by electrification / heat-pump conversion (in Canada, also cold-climate heat-pump incentive programs). Refrigeration service adds a light-commercial dimension. The sector is a popular consolidation target for private equity in both the US and Canada.
- **Growth drivers and barriers:** Drivers — aging equipment replacement, energy-efficiency rebates, new construction, recurring maintenance contracts. Barriers — skilled-tradesperson labor shortage, seasonality, exposure to housing-market and interest-rate cycles, and commodity/equipment cost inflation.
- **Competitive landscape (peer benchmarks):** Lower-middle-market HVAC/plumbing contractors in North America commonly transact in the **3x–6x SDE/EBITDA** range, with the higher end reserved for businesses that have recurring service-agreement revenue, a real management layer, and clean multi-year growth. A business at ~$327K SDE with an undisclosed contract mix would typically sit at the **lower** end of that range; the ~6.0x ask here is at the top of the band and includes — but does not separately price — the real estate.
