5. Market Analysis

Industry size and trends: US dumpster rental is a multi-billion-dollar market tied to construction and renovation activity. Roll-off / temporary-container demand tracks construction starts, remodeling, and disaster/clean-up cycles. The Tahoe Basin sees steady remodel and rebuild activity (high-value second homes, defensible-space and wildfire-driven work) supporting durable demand.

Drivers and barriers: Drivers — construction and renovation activity, wildfire mitigation and clean-up work, tourism-driven commercial turnover. Headwinds — construction cyclicality, seasonality (Tahoe winters compress the building season), landfill tipping-fee inflation, and truck/container capex.

Regulatory note: Subscription residential trash collection in the Tahoe region is franchised (South Tahoe Refuse in South Lake Tahoe; Tahoe Truckee Sierra Disposal in Truckee, franchise through ~2028). Temporary roll-off / construction-debris containers are generally outside those exclusive residential franchises, which is why an independent roll-off operator can exist here — but the broker should confirm there is no local permitting or franchise constraint on roll-off service.

Competitive landscape:

  • South Tahoe Refuse & Recycling (STR) — incumbent franchised hauler, South Lake Tahoe; broad collection plus roll-off.
  • Tahoe Truckee Sierra Disposal (TTSD) — franchised hauler for Truckee/North Tahoe; competes for roll-off and C&D work.
  • National roll-off marketplaces / brokers (Hometown, ASAP) — capacity aggregators; pressure pricing without operating assets.
  • Typical disclosed roll-off sale multiples run ~3–5x EBITDA for sub-$1M-EBITDA operators; larger platforms 4–6x+.
