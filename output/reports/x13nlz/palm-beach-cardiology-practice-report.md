## 1. Executive Summary

- **Company overview:** A solo-cardiologist private practice in Palm Beach County, FL, operating 30+ years from a 2,000 sq ft stand-alone building with 4 exam rooms. Services: stress testing (simple + nuclear), echocardiograms, peripheral vascular studies, Holter/event monitoring.
- **Financial highlights:** Annual collections grew steadily — $1.01M (2021) → $1.14M (2022) → $1.29M (2023) → $1.43M (2024), roughly +11-13%/yr. Owner's cash flow (SDE) was "over $500K" in 2023 and "strong" in 2024. Asking price $495,000 for the practice; real estate offered separately.
- **Buy Box screening summary:** **FAIL** — passes only years-in-business (✅) and top-line growth direction; fails on employee count (6 vs 10), absence of institutional EBITDA, and total owner dependence. Two criteria are unscoreable on disclosed data.
- **Why NOT pursue:**
  - It is a *job*, not a *business* — the $500K cash flow is the cardiologist's own pay. A non-physician buyer must hire a replacement cardiologist (~$588K median private-cardiology comp), which erases the earnings.
  - At 6 employees and one revenue-producing provider it is far below EarnedOut's scale floor and carries extreme key-person risk.
  - There is no EBITDA against which to test the 4.0x ceiling — the deal cannot even be screened on price.

## 5. Market Analysis

- **Industry size and trends:** US cardiology is consolidating fast — a MedAxiom survey found roughly half of private cardiology practices were under private-equity ownership by 2023, up from essentially zero a few years earlier. Solo independent practices are an increasingly rare and structurally disadvantaged segment, squeezed on payer negotiating power and on the capital needed for equipment and IT.
- **Growth drivers:** Aging population (Palm Beach County skews elderly — strong cardiology demand); steady same-store collections growth here (+11-13%/yr) shows healthy local demand.
- **Barriers / headwinds:** Medicare fee-schedule pressure, rising operating costs (medical-practice operating expenses rose ~11% in 2025), and the difficulty of recruiting a replacement cardiologist to a solo setting. Reimbursement for nuclear cardiology and imaging is subject to ongoing CMS cuts.
- **Competitive landscape (peer benchmarks):**
  - PE-backed / hospital-employed cardiology groups (e.g., regional multi-site groups in South Florida) — scale, payer leverage, recruiting pipelines; trade at 8-18x adjusted EBITDA at the platform level.
  - Small solo/small-group practices — typically change hands at ~1.5-3x SDE (a physician-replacement valuation), not an EBITDA multiple.
  - This target — $495K ask on ~$500K SDE ≈ 1.0x SDE, at the low end even of the small-practice band, consistent with a retirement succession sale to an individual cardiologist.

## 9. Risk Factors

- **Industry-specific risks:** Heavy Medicare exposure; ongoing CMS pressure on cardiology and nuclear-imaging reimbursement; consolidation leaving solo practices structurally disadvantaged.
- **Financial risks:** No disclosed institutional EBITDA; reported cash flow is SDE bundling owner-physician labor; only one SDE year quantified; no 2025 data; no FF&E or balance-sheet detail.
- **Operational risks:** Maximum key-person dependency — a single cardiologist generates 100% of clinical revenue. Replacement-physician recruiting is the single largest execution risk. Just 6 staff total.
- **Regulatory / legal:** Physician licensure (FL Board of Medicine), DEA registration, nuclear-materials handling for nuclear stress testing (state radiation-control / NRC agreement-state licensing), Stark/anti-kickback considerations on in-office ancillary services, and CMS/Medicare enrollment that must be re-credentialed for a new owner.
- **Deal-specific conditions:** Not a fit for EarnedOut's mandate. There is no scenario short of a full restructuring (hiring multiple providers and a practice administrator to build genuine absentee-owner EBITDA) that brings this inside the Buy Box, and that is a build, not an acquisition.

## 10. Outlook & Growth Opportunities

- **Strategic opportunities:** The realistic exit the broker is targeting is a retiring cardiologist selling to a younger cardiologist who wants to own a practice plus the real estate. EarnedOut is not that buyer.
