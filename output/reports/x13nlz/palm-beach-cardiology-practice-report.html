5. Market Analysis

Industry size and trends: US cardiology is consolidating fast — a MedAxiom survey found roughly half of private cardiology practices were under private-equity ownership by 2023, up from essentially zero a few years earlier. Solo independent practices are an increasingly rare, structurally disadvantaged segment.

Growth drivers: Aging population (Palm Beach County skews elderly — strong cardiology demand); steady same-store collections growth here (+11-13%/yr).

Headwinds: Medicare fee-schedule pressure, rising operating costs (medical-practice operating expenses up ~11% in 2025), difficulty recruiting a replacement cardiologist to a solo setting, and ongoing CMS cuts to nuclear-cardiology/imaging reimbursement.

Peer benchmarks: PE-backed / hospital-employed cardiology groups trade 8-18x adjusted EBITDA at the platform level. Small solo/small-group practices change hands at ~1.5-3x SDE (a physician-replacement valuation). This target at $495K on ~$500K SDE ≈ 1.0x SDE — low end even of the small-practice band, consistent with a retirement succession sale to an individual cardiologist.

9. Risk Factors

  • Industry: Heavy Medicare exposure; ongoing CMS pressure on cardiology and nuclear-imaging reimbursement; consolidation leaving solo practices structurally disadvantaged.
  • Financial: No disclosed institutional EBITDA; reported cash flow is SDE bundling owner-physician labor; only one SDE year quantified; no 2025 data; no FF&E or balance-sheet detail.
  • Operational: Maximum key-person dependency — a single cardiologist generates 100% of clinical revenue. Replacement-physician recruiting is the single largest execution risk. Just 6 staff total.
  • Regulatory / legal: FL physician licensure, DEA registration, nuclear-materials handling for nuclear stress testing (state radiation-control / NRC agreement-state licensing), Stark/anti-kickback considerations on in-office ancillary services, and CMS/Medicare re-credentialing for a new owner.
  • Deal conditions: Not a fit for EarnedOut's mandate. No scenario short of a full restructuring (hiring multiple providers and a practice administrator to build genuine absentee-owner EBITDA) brings this inside the Buy Box — and that is a build, not an acquisition.

10. Outlook & Growth Opportunities

Expansion potential: Adding a second cardiologist or APRNs could grow capacity and, over time, create real institutional EBITDA above provider compensation — but that is a multi-year operational build, not a turnkey acquisition.

Operational upside: Modernizing billing/RCM, optimizing payer mix, and expanding ancillary testing volume could lift margin — value to be created, not bought.

Strategic: The realistic exit the broker is targeting is a retiring cardiologist selling to a younger cardiologist who wants to own a practice plus real estate. EarnedOut is not that buyer.
