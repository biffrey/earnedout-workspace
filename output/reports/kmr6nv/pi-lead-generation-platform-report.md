## 4. Products & Services

- **Detailed descriptions:** Exclusive, real-time auto-accident lead generation for personal-injury attorneys (and personal-injury doctors). The proprietary system uses custom, client-branded landing pages; accident victims submit accident date, contact details, location and a representation-interest question, and qualifying leads are immediately forwarded to the attorney. Built-in follow-up sequences re-engage initially uninterested prospects.
- **Pricing model:** Flat monthly retainer by territory size — approximately $2,500 per million people of targeted population; a standard 3-million-population region runs ~$7,500/month, billed upfront. This is a recurring, territorially-exclusive subscription model.
- **Competitive differentiators:** Proprietary accident-report search methodology; "8+ years" of accident data; claimed lower cost-per-closed-lead vs. traditional PPC; exclusivity by geography; positioned as dominant in the accident-report-search niche.

## 5. Market Analysis

- **Industry size and trends:** The US personal-injury legal market is ~$61.7B in 2025 (IBISWorld), growing ~2.5% CAGR. PI advertising spend exceeded $2.5B in 2024, and PI leads command the highest CPLs in legal marketing ($100–$600+ per lead). Demand for exclusive, pre-qualified, tech-enabled leads is durable.
- **Growth drivers and barriers:** Drivers — attorneys' persistent appetite for case volume, premium pricing for exclusivity, and the data moat of an 8-year accident dataset. Barriers — Google Ads policy and cost volatility, TCPA / lead-generation regulatory scrutiny, state bar advertising rules, and a crowded competitor field.
- **Competitive landscape (peer benchmarks):**
  - Quintessa Marketing — large-scale PI lead generator; human-prescreened MVA retainers; broad national footprint.
  - On Point Legal Leads — exclusive MVA / auto-accident leads with full intake; founded ~2016.
  - 4LegalLeads — ~20-year-old exclusive auto-injury lead provider.
  - Marketing-agency M&A comps: agencies generally trade 4x–8x EBITDA; only top performers with >20% margin, low concentration and strong retention reach 8x–12x. This target's 8.1% disclosed margin sits well below that premium tier, making the 8.60x ask hard to justify.
