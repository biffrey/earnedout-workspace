## 5. Market Analysis

- **Industry size and trends:** The US FBO market is large and growing — global FBO market estimates range widely (~$23B–$35B in 2025) with CAGR estimates from ~4.4% to low double digits. Business aviation accounts for ~73% of US FBO demand, and private-jet departures sit ~15% above pre-pandemic levels. Sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) adoption and eVTOL infrastructure are emerging tailwinds. The sector is consolidating — 34+ FBO M&A transactions globally in 2023.
- **Growth drivers:** Strong business-aviation traffic, SAF demand, hangar-space shortages supporting rental pricing.
- **Barriers / headwinds:** FBO economics are dominated by fuel — a low-margin, price-volatile pass-through; profitability hinges on fuel volume, into-plane margin, and hangar occupancy. Ground-lease tenure is existential: a short or non-renewing lease can erase enterprise value.
- **Competitive landscape / valuation benchmarks:**
  - Industry practice values FBOs on a multiple of EBITDA; healthy FBOs commonly trade in the ~4x–7x EBITDA range, above EarnedOut's 4.0x ceiling.
  - National FBO chains (Signature, Atlantic, Million Air) are the consolidators; an independent single-field FBO this size would be a small tuck-in for them, not a platform.
  - At ~$149K annualized cash flow, this target is materially sub-scale versus the EBITDA levels at which FBOs are typically traded and financed.
