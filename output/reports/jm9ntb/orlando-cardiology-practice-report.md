## 1. Executive Summary

- **Company overview:** A single-physician cardiology practice in the Orlando, FL metro, operating since 1998 (~28 years). Services include simple and nuclear stress testing, echocardiograms, peripheral vascular studies, Holter/event monitoring, pacemaker management, and an anticoagulation (Coumadin) clinic. The doctor does not take hospital calls.
- **Financial highlights:** Sales ~$1,160,634 with disclosed "consistent collections" of $1.0M–$1.2M. Listed "cash flow" of $385,234 — this is Seller's Discretionary Earnings (it includes the owner-physician's compensation), not institutional EBITDA. Asking price $700,000 for the practice only; the building is owned by the seller and offered separately for purchase or lease.
- **Buy Box screening summary:** **FAIL** — only 1 of 6 hard checks passes (years in business). EBITDA and employee-count checks fail; growth, valuation, and DSCR are unverifiable.
- **Why not pursue:**
  - The "cash flow" is owner labor, not transferable enterprise earnings — after a replacement cardiologist's market salary, EBITDA is near zero. This fails the $1M EBITDA floor by a wide margin.
  - It is a sub-scale, single-provider practice — fails the ≥10-FTE test and the low-owner-dependence test; the owner *is* the business.
  - The listing restricts buyers to physicians or physician groups, structurally excluding a non-physician financial buyer like EarnedOut.

## 5. Market Analysis

- **Industry size and trends:** US cardiology is a large, growing specialty driven by an aging population. Private equity has been active in cardiology platform roll-ups (≈79 physician-practice PE deals in Q1 2025, with cardiology among the top targets). However, operating-cost pressure is intensifying — medical-practice operating expenses rose ~11.1% in 2025 while reimbursement rates continue to decline.
- **Growth drivers and barriers:** Drivers — aging demographics, demand for outpatient diagnostics, anticoagulation management. Barriers — Medicare fee-schedule cuts to cardiology and imaging, prior-authorization friction, rising labor costs, and the difficulty of recruiting cardiologists to small independent practices.
- **Competitive landscape (peer benchmarks):**
  - **Platform cardiology practices** — sell at 12–15x EBITDA; multi-physician, ancillary infrastructure (cath labs/ASC).
  - **Add-on / tuck-in cardiology** — 8–12x EBITDA when joining an existing platform.
  - **Small / single-physician practices** — 4x–8x EBITDA, but those EBITDA figures are *after* a market physician salary; this listing's "cash flow" has not had that deducted. SovDoc/FOCUS benchmarks place sub-$500K-EBITDA single-physician practices at 4.0x–6.0x EBITDA, with "owner-reliant revenue" cited as an explicit discount factor.

## 9. Risk Factors

- **Industry-specific risks:** Declining Medicare cardiology/imaging reimbursement, prior-authorization burden, and ~11% annual medical-practice cost inflation compressing margins.
- **Financial risks:** The headline $385,234 "cash flow" is SDE and bundles the owner-physician's compensation. After a market replacement-physician salary, EBITDA is near zero — the practice is not a positive-EBITDA enterprise on a financial-buyer basis. No multi-year financials disclosed.
- **Operational risks:** Maximum key-person dependency — one cardiologist drives essentially all revenue and clinical capacity. The 2–3 day/week stay-on offer is a transition aid, not a substitute for a permanent credentialed physician. No physician-independent management layer.
- **Regulatory / legal:** Medical practice — requires FL Board of Medicine licensure, DEA registration, nuclear-materials licensing for the nuclear stress lab (FL Bureau of Radiation Control / NRC agreement-state rules), CLIA, Medicare/Medicaid enrollment and credentialing transfer. Change of ownership triggers payer re-credentialing. Buyer must be a physician or physician group per the listing.
- **Deal-specific conditions:** Structurally not actionable for EarnedOut — (1) buyer-must-be-a-physician restriction excludes a non-physician acquirer; (2) EBITDA fails the $1M floor; (3) real estate is priced separately, so the $700K does not even include the building.

### B. Assumptions used for estimates

- The disclosed "cash flow" of $385,234 is treated as Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE), not EBITDA, because business-for-sale listings report owner-benefit "cash flow" inclusive of the working owner's compensation. For a single-physician practice the owner is a working cardiologist, so a market physician salary (MGMA general-cardiology range ≈ $400K–$500K+) must be deducted to reach institutional EBITDA — which yields ≈ $0 or negative.
- Employee estimate of 5–8 FTE is based on typical staffing for a single-physician outpatient cardiology practice with an in-office nuclear/echo lab (front desk, MA(s), nuclear/echo tech, biller/office manager). No headcount was disclosed.
- FF&E estimate of $150K–$400K is based on the disclosed equipment set (nuclear stress camera, echocardiography unit, Holter/event monitoring, vascular studies); no asset schedule was disclosed.
