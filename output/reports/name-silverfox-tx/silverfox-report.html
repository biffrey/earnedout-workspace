1. Executive Summary

  • Company overview: 5-year-old custom metal fabrication shop in NW Houston (11973 FM 529, Houston TX 77041) serving the Gulf Coast O&G value chain. ~85–90% skid platforms; ~10% ASME-coded pressure vessels (U-stamp + R-stamp). 100% O&G revenue. 15 FT plus 100+ 1099 contractors via 7 unaffiliated LLCs.
  • Financials: Revenue $14.94M → $15.12M → $18.56M. Normalized EBITDA $1.91M → $4.36M → $6.40M (margin 12.8% → 28.8% → 34.5%). 2026 guide: $22–25M revenue.
  • Ask: $24.367M business only (3.81x trailing); $31.567M with Waller RE (4.93x trailing).
  • Buy Box: CONDITIONAL. Passes FTEs, growth, multiple (biz only). Stretch-fails on age and EBITDA ceiling. Significant concerns: 85% concentration; excluded contractor labor; EBITDA margin spike; cyclical 100% O&G; off target-industry list.
  • Recommendation: NO LOI at current $24.4M ask. Pursue conditionally at restructured terms only (see Conclusion).

2. Company Overview

  • Founded: 2020 in NW Houston. Custom fabrication for Gulf Coast O&G, primarily skid platforms for compression packages.
  • Transition to turnkey: 2024 — brought fabrication, sandblasting, coating, painting, ASME certification, and delivery in-house. Stated driver of COGS compression (70.7% → 55.9% over 2 years).
  • Ownership: Two co-founders (names withheld in CIM). Entity per ZoomInfo: "Partnership." Confirm via TX SOS.
  • Leadership: Founders strategic only. GM + Fabrication Mgr + QA Mgr + Project Mgr + Paint Shop Mgr run day-to-day. GM positioned as CEO successor. Multiple managers have prior Baker Hughes tenure.
  • Capacity: 85,000 sqft leased production + 8,000 sqft office; 10-acre laydown; multiple overhead cranes (count discrepant between docs); 12×12×20 paint oven; 8×8×8 zinc phosphate; hydro test to 27,000 PSI; NACE I/II/III on staff; in-house trucking.
  • Balance sheet: Debt-free; $872K cash and $2.78M AR at 2025 year-end.
  • Track record claims: 5,000+ projects, 60+ customers.

6. Customers & Revenue Model

  • Segments: 100% O&G — upstream, midstream, downstream operators and service co's headquartered in/around Houston. End-markets touch global gas (US, Qatar, Australia, Canada, Saudi, Norway) via Houston-based operators.
  • Named approved clients: Enerflex, Compass, Titan, NWR, GENIS, VGAS, Innovative Compression Equipment, Exterran, TechProcure, Steel Supply LP, Encore Oilfield Services.
  • Top customer concentration: 85% in a single anchor — high-confidence: Enerflex Group (NYSE: EFXT / TSX: EFX). Enerflex acquired Exterran (also Houston-based) in Oct 2022 for $735M; both Enerflex and Exterran logos appear on Silverfox's roster. Combined, Enerflex Group is the only major dual-listed gas-compression packager in Houston, matching the CIM's "publicly traded on NYSE as well as TSX" descriptor.
  • Recurring vs one-time: 0% contractually recurring. 100% PO-based. Customer relationships repeat but engagements project-by-project; no MSAs disclosed.
  • Retention: Not quantified. Claimed "long-standing relationships" and "industry referrals."
Concentration risk note. PE/financial-buyer guidance typically flags any single customer >15% as a deal concern; deals with >25% concentration carry 15–30% valuation discounts or restructured terms (FOCUS Bankers, GP Ventures, Founders Advisors). At 85%, this becomes a single-account bet on Enerflex's continued vendor preference for Silverfox.

9. Risk Factors

  • Customer concentration (CRITICAL). 85% from one anchor (likely Enerflex Group). PE/financial-buyer norm rejects deals with single-customer >15–20%. Loss scenario destroys both DSCR and equity returns.
  • Customer relationship fragility. PO-based; no MSAs disclosed. Anchor can switch vendors at any PO cycle with no contractual penalty. Diligence must establish: 2026 PO volume, MSA/qualified-vendor status, share-of-anchor-spend, vendor-selection rationale.
  • EBITDA quality. $4.76M of 2025 adjustments. Largest single addback is $1.97M of "Tax-Saving Strategy" rent paid to related-party landlord. If buyer doesn't acquire RE and related party charges market rent, add-back may not fully transfer.
  • Labor-model risk (HIGH). 100+ contractors through 7 unowned LLCs. IRS reclassification could create 7-figure contingent payroll-tax liability; LLC owners are not selling and could renegotiate post-close; relationships likely concentrate around a small number of operators.
  • Cyclicality. Every prior O&G downcycle (2014–16, 2020) compressed compression-package CapEx 30–50%. Silverfox has never been tested.
  • Industry-target mismatch. Custom O&G metal fab not in EarnedOut's explicit target list. Closest analog: Aerospace.
  • RE bundle math. $262K/acre vs Waller $43K avg, Texas industrial $73K avg. 4 acres improved; balance is raw. Premium hard to justify outright.
  • Unaudited financials. Tax-return basis only. QofE essential.
  • Discrepancies between collateral. 3× 25-ton vs 4× 40-ton cranes; 85k vs 87k sqft facility; Brookshire vs Houston facility references.

11. Conclusion & Pursue / Pass Decision

Recommendation: NO LOI at the current $24.367M ask. Conditional pursue at restructured terms only.

The headline 3.81x trailing multiple looks attractive in isolation, but the underlying business has four overlapping risks that any one of which would normally cool a deal — and they all stack here:

  1. 85% concentration — single point of failure (Enerflex Group)
  2. 5-year track record — never tested through a downcycle, while sitting in the most cyclical end-market in the US economy
  3. EBITDA margin tripled in 24 months with $4.76M of normalization adjustments dependent on a related-party rent structure
  4. Labor delivered by 7 unowned LLCs that are excluded from the sale

Favorable items — ASME credentials, lean management, Houston location, growing top line, debt-free balance sheet, retiring sellers — are real and support continued engagement. They do not, in this analyst's view, support paying $24.4M without major structural protections.

If pursued, LOI should reflect:

  • $13.5–19M cash at close (2.5–3.5x trailing on stress-tested EBITDA)
  • $4–6M earn-out gated on Enerflex revenue retention (≥70% of 2025 anchor revenue retained in each of 2026 and 2027)
  • 15–25% seller note (3–5 year subordinated)
  • Waller real estate via 10-yr triple-net lease (~$464K/yr) — NOT outright purchase
  • R&W insurance with separate retention for 1099 misclassification exposure
  • Hard diligence conditions: QofE, named Enerflex introduction, 7 LLC assignments, Phase I, TX SOS clean

If the seller will not engage on a structured offer at this profile, the recommendation is pass. The deal as advertised is priced for a clean industrial roll-up by a strategic acquirer (e.g., a PE-backed fabrication platform), not for an independent buyer underwriting concentration risk solo.
