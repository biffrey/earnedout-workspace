## 5. Market Analysis

- **Competitive landscape (peer benchmarks):**

  - **Independent local HVAC competitors** — direct competitors of similar size in the same South Carolina market; trade at SDE multiples of ~2.5–3.5x for owner-operator shops.
  - **National franchised brands** (e.g., franchise HVAC networks) — compete on brand and lead-gen; this target appears to be an independent, not franchised.

## 9. Risk Factors

- **Industry-specific risks:** Seasonality (summer-weighted demand), refrigerant-regulation compliance costs, and skilled-labor scarcity. HVAC itself is an allowed industry — no gate risk here.
- **Financial risks:** Single-year financial disclosure only; "very profitable" is a marketing claim, not substantiated. The earnings figure is **SDE, not EBITDA** — a buyer replacing the owner with hired management would see materially lower normalized EBITDA. Reliance on subcontracted labor can compress margins if subcontractor rates rise.
- **Operational risks:** High probability of owner-operator dependence — customer relationships, estimating, and dispatch may sit with the seller. The transition period mitigates but does not eliminate key-person risk. Subcontractor reliance also raises labor-quality and availability questions.
- **Regulatory / legal:** South Carolina mechanical/HVAC contractor licensing must be verified as active and transferable; EPA Section 608 refrigerant-handling certification required for technicians. No litigation or environmental data available.
- **Deal-specific conditions:** This prospect **does not clear the Buy Box** — the controlling condition is size, not a negotiable term. No deal structure can bring $403K SDE to the $1M EBITDA floor. Recommend **pass**. If pursued anyway (outside the Buy Box), conditions would include: obtain 3-year P&Ls and tax returns, recast SDE to true EBITDA after a market-rate manager salary, confirm FTE headcount, verify license transferability, and quantify customer concentration.
