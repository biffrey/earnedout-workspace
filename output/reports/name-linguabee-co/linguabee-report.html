2. Company Overview

Background and history

Linguabee was founded in 2011 by Patrick Boudreault (a Deaf mountain biker frustrated by agency friction) and Chad W. Taylor (Bay Area technologist who built the deaf-centered booking platform). The thesis: cut out the traditional "broker" interpreting agency and let Deaf consumers select their own interpreters through a marketplace. In 2016 the company merged with Justin "Bucky" Buckhold's TIA (The Interpreting Agency) — bringing in a deaf entrepreneur who'd built a separate interpreting business after being rejected from agency staff roles for being deaf. The merger crossed $1M for the first time. Four tuck-in acquisitions since (CA 2019, IA 2019, TN 2020, CA 2022). 2022 = first $5M year. 2025 = first $10M and $15M year.

Ownership structure

Four shareholders. Legal entity: Linguabee LLC, Colorado-domiciled. CEO succession: Shareholder1 (founder era) → Shareholder1 assumed CEO 2018 → Shareholder3 (Ryan Shephard) assumed CEO 2024. Shareholder4 granted partial ownership 2025.

Key milestones

  • 2011 — Founded by Patrick Boudreault & Chad Taylor
  • 2016 — Merger with Bucky's TIA; first $1M revenue year
  • 2019 — Two tuck-in acquisitions (CA + IA)
  • 2020 — Launched pre-scheduled VRI; TN acquisition
  • 2022 — CA acquisition; major platform upgrade; signed university client needing 15+ interpreters/day; first $5M year
  • 2023 — Large corporate enterprise client begins → becomes #1 customer
  • 2024 — Ryan Shephard becomes CEO; EBITDA dips to $674K
  • 2025 — Signed national DHH K-12 program; revenue doubles to $16.4M

5. Market Analysis

The US ASL interpreting services market is highly fragmented and growing. ADA enforcement, K-12 IDEA mandates, and corporate accessibility commitments drive baseline demand. No national pure-play exceeds ~$50M revenue — Linguabee at $16-18M is meaningful scale in the niche.

Growth drivers

  • Federal/state ADA enforcement actions
  • K-12 IDEA-mandated DHH services in inclusion classrooms (Linguabee just signed one of the largest national DHH K-12 programs in 2025)
  • Corporate ESG / DEI accessibility commitments
  • Telehealth and hybrid-meeting captioning demand

Growth barriers

  • Interpreter shortage — RID-certified count is ~10K nationally vs. demand for many multiples. Wage inflation is real.
  • Localized supply markets — onsite work requires geographic interpreter density.
  • Procurement bureaucracy at education/government buyers.

Competitive landscape

  • LanguageLine Solutions — generalist LSP (Teleperformance); $1B+ revenue; ASL non-core. Comp-set multiple 12-14x.
  • Sorenson / CSDVRS — primarily VRS (subsidized by FCC TRS Fund); different model. PE-backed.
  • Birnbaum Interpreting Services (BIS) — NY metro ASL specialist; estimated $5-15M. Closest direct comp.
  • Regional ASL specialists ($2-10M range) — several PE-rolled. Transaction multiples typically 4-6x for clean assets.
Roll-up rationale for Applied Development. Applied Dev's federal-heavy portfolio (and recent $5.2M DOGE-driven cut) needs commercial + education diversification. Linguabee delivers it: Corporate 47.6% + K-12 26.6% + Post-secondary 19.1% ≈ 93% of revenue in segments Applied Dev under-indexes. The Meet & Greet identified this explicitly as the buyer thesis.
