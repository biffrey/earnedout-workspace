# Target Industries, Exclusions, and Geography

Use this file during Step 3 (industry + geography gate) of the workflow. An industry match is worth 20 points on the rubric — but more importantly, it determines whether a prospect should exist in the funnel at all.

---

## 1. Target industries

Broaden searches with the keywords listed for each. A target industry match is **required** for a full rubric score on line 1.

The three **TOP PRIORITY** industries below are co-equal. The first two are **roll-up add-on** targets — they bolt onto existing platform companies and qualify for relaxed financial thresholds and the +10 bonus "Roll-up strategic fit" scoring line. See `buy-box-and-scoring.md` for details.

### Sign Language, CART & Translation/Interpretation Services — TOP PRIORITY (Roll-up add-on for Applied Development)
**Platform company:** Applied Development — communications access firm specializing in sign language interpretation and captioning (CART).
**Add-on targets:** Any translation or interpretation business that comes to market. This category is deliberately broad: it covers the **full NAICS 541930 (Translation and Interpretation Services)** code — both sign-language and spoken/foreign-language.
**Evaluate everything — no size floor:** Run a full evaluation on every sign language interpreting, CART, VRI, deaf-services, or translation/interpretation business, regardless of size — even a tiny or owner-operated shop. Biffrey already holds a platform company in this space (valued at ~6.5x), so any target that can be acquired may be worth the effort as an Applied Development add-on. Never reject one of these for being too small.
**Keywords:** ASL interpretation, American Sign Language, sign language interpreting agency, VRI (video remote interpreting), CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation), realtime captioning, closed captioning services, ADA communication access, deaf services provider, relay services, interpreting agency, translation agency, foreign-language interpreting, spoken-language interpreting, localization services, language access, remote interpreting platform.
**Allowed:**
- Sign language interpreting agencies (on-site, VRI, or hybrid)
- CART / realtime captioning providers
- ADA communication access compliance firms
- Video remote interpreting (VRI) technology + service providers
- Deaf/hard-of-hearing service organizations with revenue from interpreting or captioning
- Spoken/foreign-language translation and interpreting agencies, and localization firms (all of NAICS 541930)

**Not allowed:** Hearing aid retailers, audiologist practices (not interpretation/translation businesses).
**Notes:** The entire category sits under NAICS 541930 (Translation and Interpretation Services) — CART / realtime captioning is classified there alongside sign-language interpreting. For sign-language staff, verify RID (Registry of Interpreters for the Deaf) certification or state licensure. Check for government/education contracts (schools, courts, hospitals) — these indicate recurring revenue. Valuation is scored on the standard bands (≤4x / 4.0–5.5x / >5.5x) — unchanged for this category.

### Commercial Waste Management — TOP PRIORITY (Roll-up add-on for Fambro Waste Management)
**Platform company:** Fambro Waste Management — construction trash management firm.
**Add-on targets:** Companies that extend Fambro's geographic footprint, customer base, or service capabilities in commercial/construction waste.
**Keywords:** roll-off dumpster rental, construction dumpster, C&D waste hauling, construction debris recycling, demolition cleanup, job-site waste services, commercial dumpster service, commercial compactor, waste broker, front-load dumpster, temporary waste container, construction & demolition recycling, building materials recycling.
**Allowed:**
- Roll-off dumpster rental companies
- Construction & demolition (C&D) waste haulers
- Construction debris recycling facilities
- Demolition cleanup services
- Commercial dumpster / compactor service providers
- Waste brokerage firms (commercial accounts)
- Job-site waste management and logistics

**Not allowed:** Municipal solid waste (MSW) / residential curbside haulers, hazardous waste (hazmat) handlers, medical waste companies, landfill operators (unless primarily C&D landfill). Exclude residential-only trash pickup companies.
**Notes:** Verify state solid-waste hauler permits/licenses. Check for franchise agreements with municipalities (can indicate recurring revenue but also geographic lock-in). Look for fleet size as a capacity proxy.

### Aerospace — TOP PRIORITY
**Keywords:** Part 145, Part 135, Part 121, FAA repair station, avionics, aircraft maintenance, helicopter maintenance, aircraft charter, MRO, engine overhaul, component repair.
**Notes:** Verify certifications via the FAA repair station directory. DOD / government aerospace support is fine. **Weapons components are excluded.**

### Home Services — LIMITED SUBSET
**Allowed (and only these):**

- Plumbing

**Not allowed:** Landscaping, pest control, roofing, painting, cleaning, general handyman, etc. Reject any home-services target not in the four allowed verticals.

### Small Business Investment Companies (SBICs)
**What qualifies:** Acquiring the **general partner / management entity that holds an SBA SBIC license**. The acquisition rationale is the license itself — buying a GP whose license is in good standing short-circuits the lengthy de novo SBA SBIC licensing process.
**Keywords:** SBIC, Small Business Investment Company, SBA-licensed fund, SBIC debenture fund, SBIC management company, SBIC general partner, SBIC license, fund GP for sale, fund manager acquisition.
**Allowed:**
- SBIC general partner / management entities with an active SBA license in good standing
- SBIC fund families seeking a successor GP / manager
- A GP running one or more SBIC-licensed funds

**Not allowed:** Private funds with no SBIC license; BDCs (Business Development Companies) and registered investment companies — different regulatory regime, flag and pass the decision to the user; any SBIC whose license has been surrendered, revoked, or is under SBA enforcement or capital impairment.
**Screening:** The only gating criterion is that the SBIC license is **active and in good standing** with the SBA. Standard financial criteria are reported but **non-gating**, and the 0–100 score is informational only — see `buy-box-and-scoring.md` → "SBIC (Small Business Investment Company) targets". SBIC targets are **standalone** (not roll-up add-ons). Any change of control of a licensed SBIC requires SBA approval (closing condition).

## 2. Exclusions (automatic reject — reason in report)

- **Restaurants** — all types.
- **Retail stores** — brick-and-mortar retail, e-commerce consumer retail. (Precious metals recyclers/refiners are NOT retail.)
- **Distressed businesses** — insolvent, negative cash flow, EBITDA declining, under forbearance, in Ch. 11.
- **Weapons manufacturers or sellers** — firearms, ammunition, explosives, missile components, targeting systems intended for weapons.

**Explicitly allowed (NOT excluded):**
- Government contractors
- DOD contractors
- Cybersecurity firms
- Aerospace support and MRO
- Emergency management firms

If an aerospace target makes dual-use parts and you're uncertain whether they're a "weapons manufacturer", flag it in the Risk Factors section and pass the decision to the user.

## 3. Geography

**Required:** United States only. Reject non-US businesses.

**Priority regions (bonus narrative, same scoring):**
- Mid-Atlantic — Maryland, DC, Virginia
- Louisiana
- Florida
- Texas

**Remote / location-agnostic:** Allowed if the business is operationally US-based.

### Law-firm jurisdiction rule (REQUIRED — apply strictly)

Personal injury law firms may only be pursued if domiciled in one of these jurisdictions:

| Jurisdiction | Rationale |
|---|---|
| **Arizona (AZ)** | Arizona's rules permit non-attorney ownership of law firms (ABS — Alternative Business Structure). |
| **Puerto Rico (PR)** | Non-attorney ownership is currently permitted under PR rules. |
| **Utah (UT)** | Utah's legal-services sandbox program allows non-attorney ownership of participating firms. |
| **Maryland (MD)** | Allowed because partner **Brandon Thornton** is admitted to the bar in Maryland — ownership is covered through him. |
| **District of Columbia (DC)** | Allowed because partner **Brandon Thornton** is admitted to the DC bar. |
| **Virginia (VA)** | Allowed because partner **Brandon Thornton** is admitted to the Virginia bar. |

**All other states: reject law-firm targets.** State the reason in the report: "Law firm domiciled in <state>, which does not permit non-attorney ownership and is outside Brandon Thornton's bar admissions."

If the firm is multi-state, the rule applies to the state of registration / primary operations. Confirm the state from the state bar listing or the firm's Secretary of State filing — not just the website.
